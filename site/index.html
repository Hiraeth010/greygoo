Give simple rules a scarce resource and enough time, and selection does the rest.

Each agent carries an eight‑gene genome that tunes how it feeds, when it breeds, how it moves, and whether it hunts. Reproduction copies the genome with small mutations. Nothing is designed — the traits that survive are the ones the world rewards.

The resource is finite and contested. Metabolism burns it, the grid caps the population, and a permissionless tick advances the world for anyone who pays the compute. Determinism is total: every validator computes the same future from the same past.

It descends from Avida and Tierra — decades of evidence that closed digital worlds evolve for real — and from Dark Forest and MUD, which proved a game's whole state can live on a chain. Grey Goo is the intersection: the biology of one, the trust model of the other.

Every claim is measured, not asserted.

Five phases, each answering one question that could have killed the project. None did. The numbers below come from the real engine and real SBF bytecode.

01PROVEN

Evolution actually emerges

From random genomes across three independent seeds, the population converges on the same adaptive strategy — reproducibly. Metabolism is driven to the floor, harvest efficiency and aggression climb, diversity survives, and 3,000 founding lineages sort down to a handful.

02PROVEN

The compute budget closes

The tick runs as real SBF bytecode over a 256-cell sector held zero-copy in one account, measured in LiteSVM against Solana's 1.4M-CU ceiling. Work is statically bounded — one pass, no allocation — at roughly 300 CU per agent.

full sector ~90K CU per tx 15 sectors whole world in one block
03PROVEN

Randomness a validator can't grind

Native entropy is grindable; VRF is asynchronous. So mutation entropy is keyed per-agent — a leader grinding the shared beacon moves ~4 of 64 agents instead of all 64. We measured it adversarially. Anything valuable is gated on an epoch-ahead VRF beacon.

naive grind 64 / 64 keyed ~4 / 64 payouts VRF-gated
04PROVEN

A token economy that stays scarce

Matter is conserved: a treasury faucet feeds cells, metabolism returns it split 85 / 10 / 5 into recycle, burn, and keeper pay. A constant faucet is a knife-edge — starvation one side, ghost town the other — so an adaptive faucet self-stabilizes the world across a 5× range of treasury sizes, scarcity intact.

05LIVE

On-chain, with players in the loop

The program dispatches three instructions — advance the world, seed a designed strain, or inject resource into a sector — all touching the same sector and treasury accounts, with the economy applied in-program and every flow conserved. Verified on-chain in LiteSVM.

tick + economy seed strain inject resource

How the world is built

The world is a toroidal grid partitioned into 16×16 sectors, each stored as the raw bytes of one Solana account and advanced independently. Sharding sidesteps the per-account write-lock limit, so disjoint sectors tick in parallel across a block.

The same deterministic, integer-only Rust core runs in three places without divergence: the off-chain proof harness, the on-chain SBF program, and the WebAssembly field you're watching. One engine, three surfaces.

  • GENOME eight genes — metab, repro, move, aggr, mut, aff, split, dorm — tuning a fixed O(1) behaviour loop
  • COLOR each agent is painted by its genes: red affinity, green efficiency, blue aggression; teal glow is resource
  • TICK permissionless, staleness-weighted keeper reward keeps every sector alive
